Implementation of Electronic Medical Records as a Step Toward Digital Hospitals in Indonesia

Meisya Ma'rifa Nur By Meisya Ma'rifa Nur January 6, 2025

The rapidly developing information technology in the current era has a significant impact on various sectors, such as the healthcare sector. In Indonesia, the implementation of Electronic Medical Records (EMR) is one of the strategic steps in achieving a fully digital hospital system. EMR is a system designed to digitize patient information and simplify clinical workflows. Through EMR, patient data will be managed digitally to replace the manual paper-based recording system. However, the success of EMR implementation in Indonesia heavily depends on several key factors, such as the readiness of information technology infrastructure, its impact on the quality of patient services, and the presence of benchmarks to assess the success of hospital digital transformation. Therefore, this essay argues that IT infrastructure readiness and the improvement of patient service quality, and the digital maturity indicators are important components that determine the success of EMR implementation as part of the digital transformation of healthcare services in Indonesia.

Information technology infrastructure is one of the key determinants of the success indicators of EMR implementation in hospitals in case it is poorly established. Infrastructure encompasses stable internet connectivity, secure data storage, interlinked hospital information systems and the availability of necessary hardware to support digital functions. Information technology infrastructure limitations in remote regions. In hospitals, poor network connectivity leads to challenges in patient data synchronization, system disruptions and slow access to medical records. Moreover, poor protection of patients’ data from cyber threats and hacking reduces public trust in EMR systems.

Hospitals should also focus on improving digital infrastructure, such as the storage of data (cloud), stable internet connectivity and systems that accommodate a large number of patients. The Ministry of Health, Republic of Indonesia, is implementing an interoperable system for both the private and public hospitals to be nationally connected. Adequately interconnected systems in exchanging patient data across healthcare facilities greatly affect what is called infrastructure readiness. Therefore, technological readiness is one of the critical factors to consider for the successful EMR implementation in Indonesian hospitals.

“EMR are not merely the replacement of paper files; they represent a significant change in healthcare management to be faster and more accurate.”

The implementation of the EMR system indeed holds great promise in enhancing the quality of healthcare services throughout Indonesia. Digitization of patient data allows medical personnel to access complete and updated health information for accurate clinical decisions. Doctors can directly access medical history, allergy records, and laboratory test results, which in turn may reduce the probability of medication errors or repetition of unnecessary examinations. Beyond this, the presence of EMR also enhances coordination and communication among healthcare providers since all members of the medical team equally have access to digitally documented patient data.

Besides that, EMR also makes the process of patient care more efficient. Through an electronic system, medical staff are free from the burden of manually filling out documents thus the time that has been saved can be used to provide more patient care. Besides, EMR presents hospitals with great potential to collect and process health data for research and public health surveillance purposes. For instance, the rapid identification of the occurrence of diseases or the effectiveness of treatments allows the taking of the most proper medical actions. Therefore, if an EMR system is utilized efficiently, it can be a great instrument for elevating the standards of healthcare and increasing patient satisfaction in Indonesia.

In order to understand how well the implementation of EMR has gone, it requires clear and measurable indicators of the process of digital transformation in hospitals. One of the very important indicators is the level of system usage by medical staff, reflecting whether the EMR is really used in daily operational activities. A high usage rate indicates that the system is considered useful, easy to operate, and sufficiently stable to support clinical work. Another equally important indicator is data accuracy and interoperability, referring to the ability of electronic data to be consistently synchronized and interconnected across service units or health facilities.

To conclude, implementing EMR in Indonesia is one of the most important steps toward a more modern and digitally connected hospital. The thesis statement itself shows that the success or failure of the implementation of EMR is highly dependent on technological readiness, benefits to hospital management in improving care for patients, and parameters for the measurement of the digitalization process. Strengthening these three aspects will make Indonesian hospitals more digitally prepared, improve healthcare services, and create more effective data integration in the future.

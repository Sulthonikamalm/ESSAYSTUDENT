The Impact of Pay-to-Win Game System on Game Balance

Ahmad Dzaki Zayyan Sugianto By Ahmad Dzaki Zayyan Sugianto January 6, 2025

Video games have grown into an important part of digital culture, and today they serve not only as entertainment but also as subjects of academic study. One interesting aspect to examine is the Pay-to-Win (P2W) system. The Pay-to-Win (P2W) system is a monetization mechanism in video games where players can use real money to gain competitive advantages, such as stronger weapons, faster access to content, or ability upgrades that directly influence game outcomes (Hamari et al., 2017). Some people argue that this system is beneficial because it provides revenue for developers and helps new players progress more quickly without spending a long time farming (Lehdonvirta, 2009). However, others believe that this system harms fairness and negatively affects the overall gameplay experience. In this context, I tend to agree with those who oppose the P2W system, because the negative impact on game balance is more significant compared to the benefits it offers from a player’s perspective. Although the Pay-to-Win system provides financial stability for developers and offers convenience for beginners who can afford to pay, its widespread use also carries the risk of creating a game environment that lacks fairness. This shows that financial profit alone is not enough without proper game design that maintains balance and ensures fair competition.

Overall, the P2W system is not entirely negative, as it offers several benefits for both developers and certain groups of players. For developers, P2W serves as a stable source of revenue, especially for free-to-play games that do not generate profit from initial sales. Through in-game transactions, developers can cover operational costs such as server maintenance, bug fixes, security improvements, and customer support. This revenue also enables them to release regular updates, seasonal events, and new content, keeping the game engaging and competitive. From the players’ perspective, P2W provides convenience for beginners to progress more quickly without spending excessive time grinding. The system also offers flexibility by allowing players to customize their playstyle through character upgrades or faster access to exclusive content. Some players even feel personal satisfaction from making such purchases, as it allows them to shape their gameplay experience according to their preferences. Therefore, despite frequent criticism, P2W continues to hold appeal and remains implemented in many popular games today.

“Financial profit alone is not enough without proper game design that maintains balance and ensures fair competition.”

While the Pay-to-Win system offers some benefits, it also brings various problems and risks that can negatively affect the overall gameplay experience. One of the most obvious impacts is the creation of unfair competition between players. In P2W games, paying players usually have stronger weapons or certain advantages that make them significantly more powerful than those who do not spend money. As a result, non-paying players often feel frustrated and left behind, even when they actually possess good skills. This situation frequently leads many players to stop playing, which eventually decreases the number of active players and can damage the game's reputation. Furthermore, player skill becomes less meaningful because victories are often determined by how much money a player spends rather than by effort or strategy (Zendle et al., 2020).

To understand how players actually perceive this unfairness, I referred to an article titled “Pay to Win or Pay to Cheat: How Players of Competitive Online Games Perceive Fairness of In-Game Purchases.” The study, conducted by Guo, Wu, Nower, and Wohn, analyzed thousands of player posts taken from public Reddit data, where many players actively share their experiences and opinions about competitive games. Their findings show that 29.47% of the posts express that players still feel the gameplay is fair or balanced even when paid features are involved. However, a nearly identical percentage 29.98% reflects dissatisfaction with the fairness of the game, especially when in-game purchases influence match outcomes. In total, the study examined 2,685 posts and comments, which served as the main dataset for further analysis (Freeman et al., 2022). These results reinforce the idea that while some players may not mind the P2W system, many others feel that it disrupts balance and reduces the overall enjoyment of playing.

Although the P2W system has various negative aspects that can potentially damage the gameplay experience, this does not mean that the system cannot be improved. Instead, this situation emphasizes the importance of efforts by game developers to create balance so that the game remains fair, competitive, and enjoyable for all players, both paying and non-paying. To achieve this goal, the implementation of paid features must be carefully designed so they do not provide excessive advantages that could disrupt competition within the game. Developers need to ensure that all players still have a reasonable opportunity to compete without facing extreme power gaps (Hamari et al., 2017). In addition, developers must actively listen to feedback from the player community to understand how the P2W system affects their gameplay experience. By taking this feedback into account, developers can make the necessary adjustments and updates to maintain overall game balance.

In conclusion, the Pay-to-Win system has both advantages and disadvantages, but its impact on fairness and competitive balance remains a major concern. Although monetization supports developers and provides convenience for new players, it also risks creating a game environment where money has more influence than player skill. Therefore, developers must prioritize balanced game design to maintain fairness and preserve player satisfaction.

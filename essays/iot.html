The Development of the Internet of Things (IoT) in Daily Life

Lidya Ammalia Suharto By Lidya Ammalia Suharto January 6, 2025

In this modern era, we live amidst rapid technological developments. One of the most talked about and used technologies is the Internet of Things (IoT). Simply put, IoT is a technology that allows various objects around us (such as lights, air conditioner(AC), watches, cars, cameras, and even kitchen appliances) to connect to the internet and communicate with each other. The presence of Internet of Things (IoT) has made many things that once seemed complicated much more practical. We can turn on lights remotely, monitor our health with a smartwatch, and use smart apps that help with our daily tasks. This technology is developing very rapidly and has entered various places from home, schools, offices, and even public facilities.

However, this rapid development has also given rise to new debates especially regarding data security and user privacy. Some people find it very helpful but others worry that their personal data could be stolen or misused. Nevertheless, one thing is certain that IoT has had a significant impact on human life. Therefore, it can be said that the development of IoT has significantly influenced daily life by increasing convenience, efficiency, and connectivity, although there are still risks that need to be considered.

“IoT not only makes tasks easier but also boosts productivity, saves resources, and supports better decision-making.”

The most obvious benefit of IoT is the increased convenience in carrying out various daily activities. Many things that previously had to be done manually can now be done more quickly and even automatically. For example, the use of smart home systems allows users to control lights, air conditioning, CCTV, or even doors using only a smartphone. With IoT, someone can turn on the air conditioning before arriving home, ensure the door is locked remotely, or set the lights to turn on automatically at a specific time. Household appliances are also becoming smarter, such as robot vacuums that can clean the house themselves or smart refrigerators that notify us when food is running low.

Besides offering convenience, the Internet of Things also plays a major role in increasing efficiency and productivity in various aspects of life. In the workplace, IoT helps companies manage operations more effectively through devices and system that can collect data automatically. For example, smart sensors in factories can detect machine performance in real time, preventing breakdown before they occur and ensuring that production runs smoothly. This not only saves time but also reduces maintenance cost.

Even though the Internet of Things brings many benefits, its rapid development also brings several challenges and risks that must be considered carefully. One of the biggest concerns is data security. Because IoT devices continuously collect and share personal information such as location, daily habits, health data or home security details, they become attractive targets for cybercriminals. If the data is not protected properly, hackers can access sensitive information, break into networks or even control connected devices. Privacy is another major issue. Many users do not realize how much of their personal data is stored and shared by IoT devices.

The development of the Internet of Things has brought major transformations to daily life by enhancing convenience, improving efficiency, and strengthening connectivity in ways that were unimaginable in the past. Through smart homes, wearable devices, workplace automation, and more advanced public services, IoT has made everyday tasks faster, easier, and more practical. However, alongside these benefits, significant challenges also arise, especially around data security, privacy protection, and the risk of becoming overly dependent on technology. With the right precautions and continuous improvement in security systems, IoT can create a future where people live more safely, more comfortably, and more efficiently.
